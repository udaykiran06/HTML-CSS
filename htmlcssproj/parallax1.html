TAJ MAHAL

section one.

The Taj Mahal meaning 'Crown Of The Palace' is an ivory-white marble mausoleum on the south bank of the Yamuna river in the Indian city of Agra. It was commissioned in 1632 by the Mughal emperor, Shah Jahan (reigned from 1628 to 1658 ) , to house the of his favourite wife, Mumtaz Mahal. This tomb is the centrepiece of a hectare (42 acre) complex, which includes a mosque and a guest house and is set in formal gardens bounded on three sides by crenellated wall.

CHARMINAR

section two.

Charminar is one of the oldest monuments in India, and it is situated in Hyderabad, Telangana. Charminar was built in 1591 and is a global symbol of Hyderabad. This monument is the official emblem of Telangana. INDIA GATE

section three.

The walls of India Gate have been inscribed with the names of the Indian soldiers who died in World War I and the Afghan Wars. The monument rises to a height of 42 meters and has many important roads spreading out from it. The arch of India Gate houses a shrine, inside which is the ever-burning Amar Jawan Jyoti.
